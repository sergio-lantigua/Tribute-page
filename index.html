Allen Iverson

Allen Iverson listening to the crowd

Here is a time line of allen iverson life:

  • 1975 - Born in Hamton Virginia

  • June 1996 - Iverson is drafted with the first overall pick of the NBA draft by the Philadelphia 76ers.

  • May 1997 - Iverson is Rookie of the Year after averaging 23.5 points, including a 50-point game against Cleveland.

  • 1997 -Iverson is arrested on drug and gun possession charges after the car he was riding in was pulled over for speeding in Virginia. He was sentenced to probation and 100 hours of community service.

  • 1998 - Two of Iverson's friends in Virginia borrowed his Mercedes and were pulled over on drug charges.Iverson was not charged.

  • 1999 - Iverson signs a six-year, $70 million contract extension with the 76ers.

  • 1999 - Iverson wins his first scoring title, averaging 26.8 points.

  • 2001 - A year after being nearly traded, Iverson wins his second scoring title and is named league MVP after averaging 31.1 points. He leads the Sixers to the NBA Finals, where they lost to the Los Angeles Lakers.

  • 2001 - Already in the middle of a 10-year, $50 million endorsement deal, Iverson signs a lifetime contract extension with Reebok.

  • 2002 - Iverson wins his third scoring title, averaging 31.4 points per game.

  • 2002 - Iverson allegedly threatens two men with a gun while looking for his wife following a domestic dispute. All 14 counts against him are later dropped or dismissed.

  • 2003 - Iverson agrees to a $76 million contract that would keep him in Philadelphia through 2009. "I always wanted to be a Sixer," Iverson said at a news conference. "I always wanted to finish my career as a Sixer."

  • 2005 - Iverson scores a career-high 60 points in a 112-99 win over the Orlando Magic. "This is the greatest performance I've ever witnessed," O'Brien says.

  • 2006 - Iverson averages 30.7 points and 7.9 assists and captures his fourth scoring title. He caps arguably his finest season by being late to "Fan Appreciation Night" at the team's final home game. Iverson and teammate Chris Webber, who were both late to the game because of injuries, later apologize.

  • 2006 - The 76ers trade Iverson and Ivan McFarlin to Denver for Andre Miller, Joe Smith and two 2007 first-round draft picks.

  • 2008 - Iverson was dealt from the Denver Nuggets to the Detroit Pistons for guard Chauncey Billups, forward Antonio McDyess and center Cheikh Samb.

  • 2009 - Iverson signed a one-year contract with the Memphis Grizzlies.

  • 2009 - Iverson made his return to Philadelphia.

  • 2013 - Iverson announced his retirement from basketball.

  • 2013 - The 76ers announced that they would officially retire Iverson's number 3.
